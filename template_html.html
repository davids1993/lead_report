SCOPE OF WORK

ENVOSPEC INC. performed a lead based paint (LBP) inspection at the aforementioned address &
apartment using a X-Ray Fluorescence Device (XRF) in accordance with recommendations in the Lead
Hazard Control Plan and in accordance with procedures outlined in HUD guidelines.
The testing was conducted by a trained inspector using a VIKEN Detection Pb200i XRF Lead Paint
Analyzer. The inspector has completed all training required by the government agencies and manufacturer
as seen on enclosed certificates.
The XRF method requires testing of selected components in each room. For example: walls, ceiling,
baseboards, doors, windows ect. The instrument is designed to measure lead content rapidly without any
damage to painted surfaces.

TESTING PROCEDURES

All testing was performed in accordance with protocol of Lead Based Paint testing in the U.S. Housing &
Urban Development (HUD) guidelines chapter 7 and NYC Childhood Lead Poisoning Prevention act of
2003 (Local Law 1 - 2004). In addition, all federal, state and city regulations governing inspection of lead
based paint in NYC were followed. A VIKEN Detection Pb200i XRF device was utilized for all tests
performed. This device has the ability to analyze and compute corrections for the differences in the
energy spectrum related to different substrates. This analysis of the energy spectrum means that the lead
paint reading displayed on the instrument already accounts for any substrate effects and no corrections are
required by an operator. This device can read through deep enough to handle virtually all painted surfaces,
but is not prone to detect lead objects located beneath or behind surfaces.

To assure that the instrument is operating properly a calibration check is done at the start and end of every
inspection. If inspection lasts four hours or more an additional calibration check is required.. Lead Paint
Standards supplied by the XRF device manufacturer (Viken) are used for the calibration checks and in
accordance with the devices Performance Characteristics Sheet (PCS).

LIMITS OF INSPECTION

Results of lead paint testing apply only to the specific areas tested at the time & date of inspection, and
the specific condition of surfaces & circumstances testing was conducted in.
If paint testing is conducted on a single particular component, it is assumed that the component tested is
representative of all like components. ENVOSPEC INC. makes no representation or warranty concerning
the standards and specifications in HUD guidelines. Paint that is inaccessible or can't be visually
inspected was not tested and may differ from those observed. It is not within the scope of this inspection
to remove surface materials and test paint that lies beneath or behind a surface. If during renovations or
demolition, any paint is discovered in areas previously not accessible or tested, it should be assumed to be
lead based paint until further testing and / or laboratory analysis is performed. The attached lead paint
inspection report is not transferable

SUMMARY

Lead Based Paint presents a serious health risk if in poor condition, including: Deterioration caused by
friction, deterioration caused by impact and deterioration caused by aging and moisture. Hazardous lead
dust can also be spread by painting and / or renovation work.
When a positive component is intact and shows no sign of deterioration it does not present a LBP hazard
(except for friction surfaces). Any worn, cracked or peeling paint should be tended to immediately.
Note that sanding or dry scraping of positive surfaces are prohibited. Contractors must be informed of any
positive surfaces that are present. Sanding and / or disturbing a positive surface by hand or by mechanical
means can spread hazardous lead dust. When positive surfaces are disturbed during renovation, it is
mandatory that paint dust and chips not be spread throughout the interior of the building and be contained
as efficiently as possible.
Following any painting, renovation or cleaning project that impacts LBP, clearance wipe sampling is
recommended immediately. Clearance wipe sampling will determine if there is lead contaminated dust
present in the area tested. Clearance wipe sampling requires the collection of dust samples from floors,
windowsills, and window wells in selected rooms or areas throughout an apartment. A certified laboratory
is utilized to analyze the collected samples for lead contamination. The dust sampling procedure acts as a
clearance test so that you can be assured that tenants are living in a lead safe environment.
Please keep this report for future reference and in case of positive components listed in this report are
disturbed in any which way.

Under federal law (24 CFR PART 35 & 40 CFR Part 745) a copy of this report must be provided to new
lessees (tenants) and/or purchasers of this property, before signing a lease or sales contract.

Landlords and sellers are also required to distribute a pamphlet approved by US EPA to ensure that
parents have information to protect their children from LBP hazards.
