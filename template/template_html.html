Lead Based Paint Executive Summary

On {{start_date[0]}} lead-based paint testing was performed on painted building components at {{inspection_address}}, Unit # {{unit_number}}.

An X-Ray Fluorescence Device (XRF) was used to test the painted components. Following you will find the results of the Lead-Based Paint inspection that was performed on these components.

Included with this report is a Lead in Paint Certification sheet. This Certification sheets contains pertinent information including but not limited to license numbers, property location, date of inspection, and instrumentation used for testing.

The enclosed Data Charts contain the results of this inspection. The important figures on the Charts are in the combined columns. The Action Level for lead in paint in New York, as set forth by the United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD), is 0.5 milligram per square centimeter (mg/cm2).

  • Results below 0.5 mg/cm2 are negative (acceptable); results equal to or above this figure are positive (unacceptable) for lead-based paint.
  • A building component displaying a positive result contains an unacceptable amount of lead in the paint and constitutes a serious health hazard if the paint is in poor condition or if it is disturbed.
  • Lead dust, when ingested or inhaled, is especially dangerous to children 6 years of age and younger.

The calibration results of the XRF device, which was used for the lead-based paint testing, are listed on the Data Charts.
The XRF calibration tests are the first six readings on the last six readings on the Standard. These tests are taken in order to ensure that the XRF device is operating and performing properly.
Components supplied by the calibration check contain a known amount of lead, which is compared to the readings.
